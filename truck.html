Machine Learning: Truck Engine Failure Prediction

Summary

The goal is to produce a classification model which predicts engine failure using supervised learning. All models can easily detect the common, negative case (no failure). To analyze and compare the different models, focus on the positive (failure) case.

Precision and recall results were computed, which demonstrate the difficulty of accurately predicting failures. The models with relatively good precision had lower recall, and vice-versa. Model selection will depend whether the particular application benefits more from reducing false positives vs. false negatives.

Data Set

Two samples were provided by Scania, a training set and a separate test set. The positive class consists of component failures for a specific component of the air pressure system (APS). The negative class consists of trucks with failures of components not related to the APS.

The datasets are anonymized and sampled from heavy Scania trucks in everyday usage. They were used for the Industrial Challenge 2016 at The 15th International Symposium on Intelligent Data Analysis (IDA).
